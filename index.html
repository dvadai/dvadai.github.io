Read our story

Our story begins at a house party in 2014. After being introduced by a mutual friend we both liked each other very much but neither of us wanted to be in a relationship at the time. We started talking and all we remember from that night is that we went home having the feeling of meeting someone special. A few days later Steve asked Dia out to go on a date. Dia was worried about an awkward date stuck with Steve for several hours, if things didn’t go well, so Dia suggested an alternative option: a short cycle trip. The planned hour long cycle date turned into a full day. This is also one of the reasons how we discovered our shared passion for cycling. Thousands of kilometers and a lot of sore backsides later we decided that we would like to tandem forever together.


The venue

The restaurant is called ‘Kisfaludy-ház Étterem’. It was originally a wine making press-house and belonged to a famous Hungarian poet, Kisfaludy Sándor. It was built in the 18th century in a Baroque style. The house has been beautifully renovated and has a fantastic view upon a hill overlooking Lake Balaton. The ceremony will be outside, on a lower terrace at the venue and the dinner will be next to it. However there is coverage from rain only at the dinner tables. If it rains, we will get to Plan B.

This is a casual informal wedding. We welcome you to wear anything you like. Just please ensure you wear something. We have friends and family all around the world with various backgrounds. Lake Balaton

The "Hungarian Sea" is the people's name for the 50-mile long lake with silky green-yellow water. Lake Balaton is a real Hungarian treasure, offering a long shoreline dotted with family resorts on the southern side and welcoming vineyards to the north.

Food and drinks

Hungarian food is delicious but heavy. If offered home made palinka (spirit) or another national favourite Unicum(like Jaegermeister but better), it is considered a bit rude to refuse. I recommend giving it a try, but feel free to not drink several litres! Just remember to drink water and eat because these drinks have a very high alcohol concentration.

Hungarian - English Survival Kit

Old people tend to speak German, young people do speak English. In general people of the Balaton area speak mostly German because of the East German tourists.


  • English [inglis]- angol [ɒn-gol]
  • Hungarian [hangerien] - magyar [mɒɟɒr]
  • Good [gud]- Jó [jo:]
  • Hi / hello / bye-bye / see you later - Szia [siɒ]
  • Yes [jesz] - Igen [igɛn]
  • No - Nem [nɛm]
  • Thank you! [Tenk ju] - Köszönöm. [køsø-nøm]
  • You are welcome! [Ju ár velkám]- Szívesen! [si:-vɛ-ʃɛn]
  • My name is… [máj ném iz...]- Az én nevem… [ɒz e:n nɛvɛm]
  • Sorry - Bocsánat [bo-t∫a:-nɒt]
  • Cheers! [csírsz] - Egészségedre! [ɛge:s-ʃe:gɛd-rɛ] (literally means: to or for your health)
  • Wine [vájn]- Bor [bor]
  • Beer [bír]- Sör [ʃør]
